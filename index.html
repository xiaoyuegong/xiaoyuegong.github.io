A STORY ABOUT LONELINESS

A Story About Loneliness is a work of Stop Motion animation I made about a pig who was so lonely that it created a friend by itself. That friend was erased and never came back to life again despite the pig's various efforts. Was that friend ever real though? Was that friend an angel or devil?


I also composed and produced the music pieces for this story about loneliness.


I especially like the character I gave the pig. How it interacts with the rest of the props is unique and likable. It is not the kind of pig who just goes straight from point to point.


I finished it the first day of being 20 years old as a birthday gift for myself and made minor modifications to it afterwards. This animation is produced with a tablet, a DSLR camera, Dragonframe, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Premiere, Adobe After Effects, Garage Band, Adobe Audition, Adobe Media Encoder and an electronic keyboard.

RHYTHMANIA

my classmate Ellie interacting with Rhythmania
the first Rhythmania prototype (paper pedal)
me giving a talk at XinCheJian
laser-cut pedal

Rhythmania is a smart pedal that knows which music to play based on the rhythm of the user stepping on it.


So if you suddenly feel the urge to dance, you need not look through the song list to pick a song that you think might be fit for the dance moves you are about to make. Just tap on the pedal with your foot to the beat you are feeling inside you, Rhythmania will automatically play the song that suits your fancy.


This interactive device is made possible by combining Processing and Arduino.


Rhythmania can play sound effects based on the intensity of the user’s dance moves. This feature is realized with web-cam. The user can add new songs to your playlist by just dragging his/her mp3 files onto the display screen and tap on the keys to define the rhythm of the song. When you press ENTER, the information about this song’s rhythm and name will be saved into a CSV file. So a new song is added to the user’s playlist and is ready to be called upon by stepping on the pedal at its defined rhythm.


Rhythmania changes the usual way of life by enabling people to dance spontaneously and assigning the task of finding music to Rhythmania, completely reversing the usual order that people pick a song first and then adjust their moves to the music.


Rhythmania is my final project for the course Computational Media. This device was featured multiple times in events and expos such as Maker Carnival at the NYU Shanghai Booth. I was also thus invited to give an introductory talk at XinCheJian, the first hackerspace in Shanghai. From there, I met a lot of people, got my first part time job and became familiar with the maker/hacker scene in China.

AutoLantern is a rabbit lantern that knows to turn on itself when the environment is dark.


This is an interactive toy I designed for MakeForKids Workshops to introduce Arduino to children. By trying to complete an AutoLantern by themselves, children can learn about the basics of electronics and Arduino as well as practice their handcrafts.


This lantern is made light and convenient with Arduino Nano.


ANTIPROCRASTINATOR

Antiprocrastinator is an web app I built to help college students with overcoming procrastination.


Antiprocrastinator is basically an event manager that enables the user to set up a goal for each day and set long-term and short-term project reminders. The user can define the importance and the deadline of each project and keep track of them.


This project is accomplished with HTML, Javascript, jQuery Mobile, PHP and MySQL.

COLORDIGGER

IMA Show
IMA Show

ColorDigger is a game designed to test and enhance sensitivity to colors and instant memory.


Colored circles will appear on the map for a couple of seconds as the game starts. Then the circles disappear and the bear ColorDigger need to dig them out of the map.


The colors of the circles are generated randomly; as ColorDigger levels up, there will more colors, among which the contrast will diminish and more accuracy in color recognition is required.


The player has to memorize the positions of the circles; then the player needs to check the price tags on the right and decide instantly how to gain as much money as possible in the limited time, taken into consideration the prices and distances of the circles.


The initial position of ColorDigger is also random. The player cannot remember just a few of the circles close to the starting position of ColorDigger. Instead, the player will have to get a quick sense of the general distributions of the circles.


As ColorDigger levels up, the time to look at the map decreases; the circles get smaller; the accuracy of positioning required to dig out the colors increases; the number of colors increases; the similarity between some of the colors increases; the minimum income to keep ColorDigger alive increases.


This is a web game built with HTML, Javascript. jQeury Mobile and Processing. My friends and I have fun playing it.

Hungerace is a funny video that depicts the perspectives of three students, two of which are racing.
